Six Thinking Hats Reflection

White Hat: Focusing on Facts

When discussing the StarRail website project, we looked at the factual information that needed to be included. My partner and I decided the site should organize information about legendary and epic characters, including their abilities, roles, and elemental powers. We also discussed reliable sources like the official StarRail site and fandom wiki to ensure that the information is accurate and up to date.

Red Hat: Feelings and Intuition

Emotionally, I felt excited about this project because StarRail is a game I enjoy and know well. My partner shared that the idea could really help new players feel more confident and welcomed into the community. We both agreed that the site should have a visually appealing design that reflects the game’s space-fantasy theme, making it feel immersive and fun.

Black Hat: Judgement and Caution

During the Black Hat discussion, we talked about possible challenges. One issue might be keeping the data current since new characters and updates are released frequently. Another concern is organizing the large amount of content in a way that doesn’t overwhelm visitors. We also discussed copyright—making sure we don’t use official images without permission.

Yellow Hat: Optimism and Value

With the Yellow Hat, we focused on why the project could succeed. The website could serve as a go-to resource for both new and experienced players. By combining clear visuals, character summaries, and quick access to gameplay tips, it could stand out as both informative and user-friendly. We also felt that the site could build community by linking to fan art and guides.

Green Hat: Creativity and New Ideas

As the Green Hat thinker, I suggested adding interactive features such as a character filter where users can sort by type, rarity, or element. We also thought of creating short animations or hover effects for each character card to make the site feel more dynamic. This hat encouraged us to take creative risks and make the site not just informative, but also exciting to explore.

Blue Hat: Organization and Overview

Finally, with the Blue Hat, we reviewed how all the other ideas fit together. We decided to build the site using organized sections—one for characters, another for gameplay tips, and another for updates.
